Tarsius: The Tiny Primate with Big Eyes

Scientific Name: Tarsius

Tarsiers are found in the **forests of Southeast Asia**, particularly in the Philippines, Borneo, and Sumatra. They thrive in **tropical rainforests** and are often spotted in dense vegetation near rivers or streams. These tiny primates prefer areas with plenty of trees, as they are arboreal and rely on branches for movement and shelter.

Due to their small size and nocturnal nature, tarsiers are difficult to spot in the wild. They are highly territorial and tend to stay within a small home range, rarely venturing far from their preferred habitat.

Tarsiers are known for their **enormous eyes**, which are each larger than their brain! These eyes are adapted for excellent night vision, allowing them to hunt insects in the dark. They also have **long hind limbs** and **fingers with padded tips**, which help them leap between trees with incredible precision.

Despite their small size (about **10-15 cm in length**), tarsiers are skilled hunters. They have sharp teeth and claws, which they use to catch and consume insects, small birds, and even bats.

Tarsiers are **carnivorous** and primarily feed on insects, such as crickets, beetles, and grasshoppers. They are also known to prey on small vertebrates like birds, bats, and lizards. Their hunting technique involves **silently stalking their prey** and then pouncing with a sudden leap.

Interestingly, tarsiers do not drink water directly. Instead, they obtain all the moisture they need from their prey, making them highly adapted to their environment.
